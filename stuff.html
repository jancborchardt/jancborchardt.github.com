Everything I own

Fits in a carry-on backpack and is all black. I try to strike a balance between quality and sustainability.
This stuff is reasonably good for all-weather, and works for -10° C snowy Europe as well as 30° sunny Southeast Asia.

Tech

  • Laptop

    Needs to be light, thin and have a great battery life. The Purism Librem 13 has great design & build quality, hardware switches for camera/microphone & wireless, and it runs nicely with Ubuntu + GNOME.
    Otherwise the Tuxedo InfinityBook seems good as well.
    Still looking forward to more fair production processes in laptops too.

  • Phone

    The Fairphone 2 of course. It’s fairly produced and components can be switched out easily. As operating system I use LineageOS without Google. Apps I get through F-Droid.
    The phone plan is from goood.

  • Earphones

    The LSTN Avalon are nice in-ear headphones made from wood. The plug is weirdly angled and quite big though, and the buds make a strange noise when put in. They are great for noise-isolation and silence when sleeping.

  • Screen

    The AOC I2475PXQU is a very nice adjustable external 24" display for working. It is basically the only monitor I could find which is matt and fulfilled Energy Star, TCO & EPEAT Gold.

Clothes

  • Shirts

    3 Formal Friday Jean mulesing free merino t-shirts. Merino because it’s anti-bacterial and does not smell easily, and these ones are a bit longer so great fit for cycling too. Does come at an increased price though, but that’s why I only have 3.

  • Underpants

    Mulesing-free merino underpants from Kaipara. These are the best merino boxer shorts I found: fully black, no logo or top rim, and great fit!

  • Pants

    One pair of Nudie Jeans Lean Dean dry everblack and one pair of Nudie Jeans Slim Adam chinos.
    And one pair of Prana Hybridizer shorts which can also be used for sports and swimming.

  • Jacket

    Mammut Ultimate is really good, light and I find myself wearing it all the time. (I have an older version. Glued the side zippers to stay put on the bottom part, and painted the logos black with textile marker.)
    I will switch to the Patagonia Levitation Hoody when the other jacket is worn down since their ethics and production practices are better.

  • Shoes

    Vivobarefoot Ra II are my only closed shoe no matter the season.
    As sandals I use Bedrock Classic. (Might try the Monk Sandals Monte Silentii as they are based in Europe.)

Hygiene

  • Toothbrush

    I use bamboo brushes because they are less bad for the environment than plastic ones. The Hydrophil bamboo brush is pretty nice. With it Neobio fluoride toothpaste which is one of the only well-rated organic toothpastes.

  • Soap

    For showering I use a Dr. Bronner’s eukalyptus soap (the one with the brown label). Can also be used as shaving cream and is fully biodegradable.

  • Deodorant

    The i+m deo roll-on is the only aluminum-free and organic deodorant I found which works. (Downsides are that it’s expensive and I prefer spray because there’s lighter and smaller containers.)

  • Shave, hair & nails

    Standard Razors are really nice safety razors. Much better than a cartridge razor and less wasteful. Any regular safety razor is good too.
    Haircuts I do myself with barber scissors.
    For nails I have a simple metal nail clipper.

Tools

  • Backpack

    The pinqponq Cubik Medium has 19l of space, good compartments, looks great and is made of recycled materials.
    Otherwise the Haglöfs Corker 18l is a great backpack I used for a long time.

  • Money

    Bank account at GLS bank. They are a sustainable and ecological bank. Investments are only made in companies with proper standards. Also see Fair Finance Guide and Facing Finance.

  • ID

    I rarely drive but the drivers license is good as small photo ID. And a passport for traveling. A national ID is not necessary.

  • Health

    Passes for insurance, vaccination, radiology & organ donation. Also register for bone marrow donation at DKMS.

  • Bed

    Kiezbett is a really nice bed, regionally and sustainably produced from Berlin. Otherwise cardboard furniture seems to be a great and affordable alternative.

  • Bicycle

    I love to bike and my Cinelli Gazzetta 2016 track bike is great fun. 😊 My next bike will be a bamboo bike like the one by Stark or a self-made at OZON Cyclery.
    Since chain lube is based on petroleum, use the biodegradable chain lube by Orontas.
